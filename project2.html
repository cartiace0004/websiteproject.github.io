Employee Attrition Exploratory Data Analysis

Employee Attrition refers to the gradual reduction of a company's workforce that occurs when employees leave the organization and are not replaced. It differs from turnover in that attrition typically implies a long-term reduction in headcount, rather than a quick replacement of the departing employee.

High attrition rates can signal underlying issues such as job dissatisfaction or poor management, impacting a company's productivity and profitability. This project is dedicated to understanding the key drivers of attrition by analyzing various factors, including Department, Age, Job Satisfaction, Income, and Tenure. Our goal is to uncover meaningful patterns and provide data-driven insights that can be used to develop effective strategies for improving employee retention.
